Can I still drink while taking this?

Yes. This is built around the assumption that you're not going to quit, and that you shouldn't have to. Silymarin supports your liver's normal function while it does its daily work — including processing alcohol. Most men in this category drink 1–3 a day and have for decades. The product is designed for that reality, not against it.

How is this different from the milk thistle at CVS or Costco?

Concentration. The clinical research on milk thistle is almost entirely run on extract standardised to 80% silymarin. Most pharmacy and warehouse bottles use 30–50%, or aren't standardised at all, which means the active compound concentration can vary wildly bottle to bottle. Below 80%, the supportive effect drops off sharply.

When will I feel a difference?

Most men notice changes between week 3 and week 6 — usually the afternoon fog lifting first, then the morning puffiness. The work happens at the cellular level, so the first two weeks tend to feel like nothing. That's not the product failing. That's the product doing what it's supposed to do, internally, before you can see it.

Is it safe to take with my medications?

Milk thistle is one of the most studied supplements in this category and is generally considered safe. That said, if you take prescription medication — especially statins, blood thinners, or anything for blood sugar — talk to your doctor before adding any new supplement.
